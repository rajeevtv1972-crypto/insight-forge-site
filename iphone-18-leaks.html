iPhone 18 Leaks: The "Split" Launch, The Foldable, and the End of the "Air"?

Published: January 26, 2026

The world of Apple leaks never sleeps. We are barely into 2026, the iPhone 17 series is still fresh in our pockets, and yet the rumor mill for the iPhone 18 is already spinning at maximum velocity.

If the latest reports are to be believed, Apple is planning one of the most radical shifts in its release strategy in a decade. We aren't just looking at spec bumps; we're looking at a completely new timeline, a potential foldable debut, and a fascinating "post-mortem" for the experimental iPhone "Air" lineup.

Here is a deep dive into everything we know so far about the iPhone 18, based on the credible whispers from the supply chain.

1. The "Split Launch" Strategy

For years, September has been the iPhone month. You get the standard models, the Pro models, and the entire lineup hits the shelves at roughly the same time. According to multiple sources, 2026 might break this tradition.

The Rumor:

Apple is considering a split launch window. The high-end models — the iPhone 18 Pro, 18 Pro Max, and the new Foldable — would launch in Fall 2026 as usual. However, the standard iPhone 18 and the new budget model, the iPhone 18e, could be delayed until Spring 2027.

Why it might happen:

This strategy could help Apple better manage production, reduce supply chain pressure, and create two major sales spikes per year instead of one.

2. The Foldable Finally Arrives

Perhaps the biggest leak of all is the long-rumored foldable iPhone finally becoming real.

The Rumor:

Apple is testing a book-style foldable with a near-crease-free inner display and a titanium frame. Early prototypes reportedly feature an 8-inch unfolded screen, placing it directly against foldables from Samsung and Google.

Why it matters:

This would mark Apple's first entry into the foldable market, a category it has watched cautiously for years.

3. Under-Display Face ID

Apple is reportedly experimenting with hiding Face ID sensors under the display for a cleaner, uninterrupted screen.

The Rumor:

The Dynamic Island could shrink significantly or disappear entirely on Pro models, with Face ID and proximity sensors hidden beneath the OLED panel.

Impact:

This would allow Apple to offer a truly edge-to-edge display for the first time.

4. Variable Aperture Cameras

Camera innovation remains central to Apple's roadmap.

The Rumor:

Apple is testing variable aperture lenses, allowing the camera to adjust light intake dynamically depending on the scene.

Why it matters:

This could dramatically improve low-light photography and depth control without relying purely on software.

5. The Fate of the Standard iPhone

With the split launch, the standard iPhone 18 could become a spring product rather than a fall one.

The Rumor:

The base iPhone 18 may launch alongside the iPhone 18e in early 2027, separating it from the Pro cycle entirely.

6. RIP iPhone Air?

The iPhone 17 "Air" (or Slim) was Apple's attempt to prioritize thinness and lightness over battery and performance. However, early data suggests sales were "below expectations."

The Rumor:

Apple may be scrapping the "Air" branding entirely for the iPhone 18, while the "budget" slot is filled by the new iPhone 18e.

Verdict: A Year of Transition?

If these leaks hold true, the iPhone 18 cycle represents a massive shift in Apple's product strategy: a premium-focused fall launch for the enthusiasts, and a delayed standard launch for the masses.

Should You Wait?

If you want the Foldable or the Variable Aperture camera, 2026 is shaping up to be a very exciting year. However, the rumored Spring 2027 release date for the standard models might feel like a long time to wait.

Disclaimer: These are leaks and rumors as of January 2026. Apple's plans can, and often do, change.
